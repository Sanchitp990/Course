# Lecture - 01 | Quick Introduction to Unreal Engine 5


## What is Unreal Engine (UE)

**Unreal Engine** (**UE**) is a 3D computer graphics *[game engine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Game_engine)* developed by *Epic Games*, initially made for use in the 1998 first-person shooter video game Unreal. Originally developed for PC first-person shooters, it has since been used in a variety of genres of games and has been adopted by other industries, most notably the film and television industry.

## History of Unreal Engine 5

Unreal Engine, started by **Tim Sweeney** in 1995 for Epic Games' Unreal (1998), revolutionized 3D gaming with features like real-time rendering, dynamic lighting, and C++ scripting, evolving from UE1 (software-based) to UE2 (GPU-focused), UE3 (console/mobile power, BioShock, Gears of War), and UE4 (Fortnite, PUBG), culminating in UE5's photorealistic Nanite (virtualized geometry) and Lumen (real-time global illumination), pushing boundaries for games, film, and simulation with advanced tools for creators.
